SEBI INVESTOR GUIDANCE NOTES (SUMMARY)

Investors should be aware that mutual fund and equity investments are
subject to market risk, and past performance is not indicative of future
returns. Before investing, an investor should read all scheme-related
documents carefully, including the Scheme Information Document (SID) and
Key Information Memorandum (KIM).

Exit loads may apply if units are redeemed before a specified holding
period; these are disclosed upfront in the scheme documents and are
intended to discourage short-term churn that can hurt long-term investors
in the same scheme.

SEBI requires that risk-o-meters be disclosed for every mutual fund scheme,
categorizing schemes from "Low" to "Very High" risk based on the
volatility and credit quality of the underlying portfolio. Investors are
expected to match their own risk appetite and investment horizon to the
risk category of the scheme or strategy they choose, rather than chasing
recent high returns without considering volatility.

Algorithmic and quantitative strategies that rely on historical data and
statistical models, including machine learning predictions, are not
guarantees of future performance. Backtested results can be subject to
overfitting and survivorship bias, and investors should treat backtests as
illustrative rather than predictive.
