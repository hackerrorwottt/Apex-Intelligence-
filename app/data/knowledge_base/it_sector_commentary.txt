IT SECTOR ANNUAL REPORT COMMENTARY (ILLUSTRATIVE)

Revenue grew 15% year-on-year in constant currency terms, driven by strong
demand in BFSI (banking, financial services, and insurance) and retail
verticals, despite continued macroeconomic uncertainty in key export
markets such as North America and Europe.

The company maintained a healthy cash flow position, with operating cash
flow conversion remaining above 90% of net profit for the fiscal year,
reflecting disciplined working capital management and a low-debt balance
sheet. Free cash flow generation continued to support both dividend payouts
and share buybacks without requiring incremental borrowing.

Management's industry outlook remains positive, citing continued client
spending on cloud migration, AI-led automation, and cybersecurity
modernization as durable multi-year demand drivers for IT services
exporters. Margins are expected to remain broadly stable, supported by
pyramid optimization and currency tailwinds, even as wage inflation in
select geographies puts modest pressure on operating margins.

Attrition rates have normalized to pre-pandemic levels, easing pressure on
employee costs, and the company highlighted continued investment in
reskilling its workforce toward generative AI and cloud-native engineering
capabilities as a strategic priority for sustaining revenue growth.
