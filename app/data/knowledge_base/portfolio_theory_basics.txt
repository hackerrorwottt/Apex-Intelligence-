PORTFOLIO DIVERSIFICATION AND RISK MANAGEMENT

Diversification reduces unsystematic risk by spreading capital across assets
whose returns are not perfectly correlated. When one holding underperforms,
gains elsewhere in the portfolio can offset the loss, smoothing overall
returns without necessarily lowering expected return.

Gold and gold ETFs are commonly used as a hedge against equity market
drawdowns and currency depreciation. Gold tends to have low or negative
correlation with equities during periods of market stress, which makes a
small allocation to a Gold ETF useful for reducing portfolio volatility,
even though gold itself does not generate cash flow or earnings growth.

Modern Portfolio Theory, introduced by Harry Markowitz, formalizes this
intuition: for a given level of expected return, there exists a combination
of assets that minimizes portfolio variance. Portfolios lying on the
Efficient Frontier offer the best possible expected return for their level
of risk. The Maximum Sharpe Ratio portfolio is the point on this frontier
that maximizes risk-adjusted return relative to a risk-free rate.

A well-diversified equity portfolio for a moderate-risk investor typically
spans multiple sectors (technology, financials, energy, consumer, and
industrials) rather than concentrating in a single industry, since sector-
specific shocks (regulatory changes, commodity price swings, demand cycles)
affect different industries at different times.
